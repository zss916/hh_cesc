I. Scope of Application

This Privacy Policy applies to all services we provide you through this Application, including remote monitoring of energy storage systems, power station management, data query, alarm notification, etc.

This Policy only applies to the information collected and processed by us ourselves, and does not apply to any information collected and processed by any third party through its own channels, nor to the third-party platforms/services linked in this Application.

II. Legal Basis for Data Processing

In accordance with Article 6 of the GDPR, we process your information based on the following legal grounds:

Necessity for Contract Performance: Necessary for providing you with core services such as energy storage monitoring and power station management, and for performing the cooperation contract between the two parties;

Your Explicit Consent: For information processing related to non-core functions (such as sharing data with third-party service providers), we will obtain your explicit consent, and you have the right to withdraw such consent at any time;

Pursuit of Legitimate Interests: Information processing necessary for optimizing product functions, improving service quality and ensuring system security on the premise of not impairing your basic rights and freedoms;

Performance of Statutory Obligations: Processing relevant information in accordance with the mandatory requirements of laws and regulations, judicial organs or administrative competent departments.

III. Information We Collect and Use

(I) Types of Collected Information

To provide you with a complete and high-quality energy storage monitoring and management service, we strictly follow the GDPR principle of "data minimisation" and only collect the information necessary for the provision of services, which is divided into two categories: personal information and non-personal information related to power stations and equipment, as follows:

Personal Information: Only basic information necessary for completing account identity identification and service communication is collected, including your name, registered email address and contact number, which is uniformly entered into the system by the administrator. Among them, online identity identification-related information (such as IP address, device identifier) will be regarded as personal data and protected in accordance with the GDPR requirements when it can identify a specific natural person.

Non-personal Information Related to Power Stations and Equipment: Core business data collected for realizing monitoring, management and analysis functions, including but not limited to:

Operation Data: Charging and discharging records of energy storage systems, photovoltaic power generation data, real-time equipment status, SOC status, power curves, efficiency analysis data;

Log Data: Alarm logs, equipment operation logs, system access logs;

Environmental and Benefit Data: CO₂ emission reduction, equivalent standard coal savings, charging income, discharging income, photovoltaic power generation income and other operational data;

Basic Equipment Information: Equipment model, serial number, installation location, configuration parameters, hardware version information.

Equipment and Network Log Information: To ensure system security and optimize service experience, this Application will automatically collect a small amount of equipment and network-related information, including: the model of the mobile device you use, operating system version, network type, IP address, operation logs and abnormal error logs of this Application. Such information is only used for system fault troubleshooting and service optimization, will not be analyzed in association with your personal information, and will be anonymized in accordance with the GDPR requirements.

(II) Methods of Information Collection

Personal Information: Uniformly entered into the Application system by the Company's administrator according to your authorized information, with no user-independent registration and entry link;

Non-personal Information Related to Power Stations and Equipment: Automatically collected by energy storage equipment and uploaded to the server of the corresponding region of this Application, without the need for manual entry by you;

Equipment and Network Log Information: Automatically collected by this Application during operation, with no manual intervention in the collection process.

(III) Purposes of Information Use

The information we collect is only used to provide you with energy storage monitoring and management services, and the specific use scenarios include:

Provide you with the core functions of this Application, including real-time monitoring of the operating status of energy storage power stations, viewing energy data, managing alarm information, analyzing operational benefits, exporting data reports, etc.;

Generate professional operational analysis reports and statistical data reports to provide data support for your power station operation and maintenance decisions;

Optimize the performance and operation and maintenance strategies of energy storage systems, push equipment fault alarms and abnormal early warning information in a timely manner, and ensure the safe and stable operation of power stations;

Calculate and display the environmental value (such as carbon emission reduction and energy saving data) and economic benefits of power stations to provide a reference for your operation and management;

Improve and optimize the product functions and service experience of this Application, and repair system vulnerabilities and troubleshoot operation faults;

Complete the necessary information retention and compliance review in accordance with the requirements of laws and regulations or the provisions of this Policy.

We promise: we will not use the collected information for purposes not explicitly agreed in this Policy. If it is indeed necessary to use it for other purposes, we will explain it to you in advance and obtain your explicit consent.

IV. Information Storage

(I) Storage Location

According to your use region and cooperation agreement, we will store all your information (including personal information and power station and equipment-related data) on the server in your region. Unless the GDPR cross-border data transfer requirements are met, the data will not be transferred to regions within China or other regions not recognized as adequate.

(II) Storage Period

In accordance with the GDPR principle of "storage limitation", we only retain your information for the period necessary to achieve the service purpose:

Personal Information: Stored until 12 months after the cancellation of your account/termination of the service cooperation, after which your personal information will be deleted or anonymized;

Non-personal Information Related to Power Stations and Equipment: Stored until 36 months after the termination of the cooperative relationship between you and the Company, after which it will be deleted or anonymized according to your needs or the requirements of laws and regulations;

Equipment and Network Log Information: Stored for 6 months and automatically deleted upon expiration.

If the storage period needs to be extended due to the requirements of laws and regulations, judicial investigations, dispute resolution and other reasons, we will retain the information within the statutory scope and delete or anonymize it in a timely manner after the relevant matters are handled.

(III) Storage Security

We adopt multi-level and full-process security protection measures to ensure the security of your information during storage and transmission, and the specific measures include:

Data Transmission: Adopt TLS/SSL encryption protocol to ensure that all information is not stolen or tampered with during transmission;

Data Storage: The database adopts industry-standard encrypted storage methods (including pseudonymization), sets strict access control permissions, and only authorized personnel can access relevant information;

System Security: Deploy firewalls and intrusion detection systems, conduct regular security audits, vulnerability scans and system hardening to prevent hacker attacks and data leakage;

Personnel Management: Conduct strict confidentiality training and permission management for employees who have access to information, sign confidentiality agreements, and formulate severe penalties for irregular operations;

Security Testing: Regularly test, evaluate and optimize the effectiveness of security measures to ensure compliance with the GDPR data security requirements.

V. Cross-border Data Transfer

If cross-border data transfer is involved due to business needs, we will strictly follow the provisions of Articles 44-49 of the GDPR and only conduct it when any of the following conditions is met:

The receiving country has obtained the "adequacy decision" recognition of the European Commission (such as Andorra, Argentina, Canada, New Zealand, Switzerland, etc.);

Appropriate safeguards recognized by the GDPR have been adopted, including signing the Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission and implementing Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs), etc.;

Obtained your explicit consent and fully informed you of the purpose of transfer, the recipient, potential risks and your rights;

Comply with the cross-border transfer exemption provisions stipulated by the GDPR (such as necessary for contract performance, protection of the vital interests of the data subject, etc.).

We will conduct a comprehensive assessment of the legality of cross-border data transfer and keep relevant records to ensure data security during the transfer process.

VI. Information Sharing, Transfer and Public Disclosure

We attach great importance to the privacy of your information and will not sell or rent any of your personal information, nor will we randomly share, transfer or publicly disclose the information, which will only be processed in a limited manner under the following statutory or necessary circumstances:

(I) Information Sharing

We will share non-personal information (power station and equipment operation data) with partners who meet the data security requirements only within the scope necessary for providing you with energy storage monitoring services, and will not share any personal information. The specific partners include equipment manufacturers, system integrators, etc. We will sign a strict Data Confidentiality Agreement and a data processing agreement (DPA) required by the GDPR with partners, clarify the rights and obligations of both parties, require them to process data in accordance with this Policy and relevant laws and regulations, not use the data for any other purposes, and accept our supervision and audit.

(II) Information Transfer

Without your explicit consent, we will not transfer your personal information to any third party; if it is necessary to transfer personal information due to the Company's merger, division, acquisition, bankruptcy liquidation and other reasons, we will inform you in advance of the transferee's name, contact information and data processing method. The transferee will continue to perform the information protection obligations agreed in this Policy. If the transferee changes the purpose of data processing, it will re-obtain your explicit consent.

(III) Public Disclosure

We will not publicly disclose your personal information and power station and equipment-related data, and will only make limited disclosure under the following circumstances:

Disclose necessary information in accordance with the mandatory requirements of laws and regulations, judicial organs and administrative competent departments to cooperate with investigations, evidence collection, law enforcement and other work;

Disclose relevant information within a reasonable scope to protect your life and property safety or public interests;

Information that you have explicitly agreed to disclose publicly.

(IV) Use of Aggregated Data

We may use aggregated data (information that cannot identify a specific individual, power station or equipment) that has been de-identified and anonymized for industry analysis, product research and development, service optimization and scientific research. The use of such aggregated data does not need to be separately informed to you and will not cause any impact on your privacy.

VII. Your Rights as a Data Subject

In accordance with the provisions of Articles 15-22 of the GDPR, as a data subject, you enjoy the following rights, and we will provide you with convenient channels for exercising such rights:

Right of Access: You have the right to confirm with us whether your personal information is being processed, and request to obtain details such as the purpose, category, recipient and storage period of the information processing, as well as a copy of the personal information (provided in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format);

Right of Rectification: If you find that your personal information is incorrect or incomplete, you have the right to request us to rectify or supplement it in a timely manner;

Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): You have the right to request us to erase your personal information under the following circumstances: ① the purpose of information processing has been achieved or is no longer necessary; ② you withdraw your consent and there is no other legal basis for processing; ③ the information processing act is illegal; ④ laws and regulations require erasure. We will respond within a reasonable period and notify the third parties who have obtained the information to cooperate with the erasure;

Right to Restriction of Processing: You have the right to request us to restrict information processing under the following circumstances: ① you question the accuracy of the information and we are verifying it; ② the information processing is illegal but you do not wish to have it erased; ③ we no longer have the need to process it but you need it for legal claims; ④ you object to the processing and we are verifying the legality. During the restriction period, we may only store the information and shall not use it for other purposes;

Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request us to export your personal information in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format, or transmit it directly to another data controller designated by you (if technically feasible);

Right to Object: You have the right to object to information processing based on legitimate interests or public interests, as well as information processing for direct marketing. We will stop the relevant processing after receiving the objection (unless we can prove that there is a legal basis that takes precedence over your rights);

Right to Withdraw Consent: You have the right to withdraw your consent to information processing at any time. After the withdrawal, we will stop the relevant information processing, but this will not affect the legal processing acts that have been carried out based on your consent before;

Right to Lodge a Complaint: If you believe that our information processing act violates the GDPR or this Policy, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the data protection supervisory authority in your region.

Method of Exercise: You may submit a request for exercising your rights through the contact information at the end of this Policy. We may require you to provide necessary identity verification materials (to ensure information security) and reply and process it within 15 working days. We will not charge fees for reasonable requests; for repeated, excessive or malicious requests, we may charge reasonable cost fees or refuse them.

If it is found that minors' information is entered by mistake, we will delete it in a timely manner; if it is indeed necessary to process minors' information (such as in the case of statutory authorization), the explicit consent of their parents or guardians will be obtained.
